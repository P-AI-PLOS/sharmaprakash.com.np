## Cluster A — Strategy

### Blockbuster vs Netflix (myth-debunk angle)
- Real arc: 2000, Netflix (burning cash) offered ITSELF to Blockbuster for $50M; Antioco declined. Blockbuster's Total Access (2006–07) was actually beating Netflix until the Icahn board fight, franchisee economics, and $1B debt killed the counterattack. Chapter 11: Sept 2010.
- Myth to debunk: "arrogant Blockbuster laughed and died." The better Rumelt story: they briefly HAD a coherent strategy, then abandoned it — strategy without commitment.
- Sources: Inc. (Randolph firsthand), Newsweek fact-check, Antioco's 2011 HBR "How I Did It", TodayIFoundOut debunk.
  - https://www.inc.com/minda-zetlin/netflix-blockbuster-meeting-marc-randolph-reed-hastings-john-antioco.html
  - https://www.newsweek.com/fact-check-did-blockbuster-turn-down-chance-buy-netflix-50-million-1575557

### Microsoft/Nokia — $7.6B write-off (bad strategy: goals mistaken for strategy)
- 2013 acquisition ~$7.2–7.9B → July 8, 2015: $7.6B impairment (bigger than purchase price) + 7,800 layoffs. Largest write-off in MSFT history.
- Pin to Microsoft's July 8, 2015 8-K for tier 1.
- https://techcrunch.com/2015/07/08/microsoft-writes-down-7-6b-of-its-nokia-acquisition-announces-7800-layoffs

### Kodak — capabilities without a cascade
- Sasson prototype 1975; 1.4MP CCD record 1986; Kodak invested billions in digital, was ~#1 US digital camera seller ~2005, still bankrupt Jan 19, 2012 — because where-to-play/how-to-win never cascaded into a digital business model (film razor-blade economics).
- Myth to debunk: "Kodak ignored digital" (Snopes: false).
- https://www.snopes.com/fact-check/kodak-digital-camera-invention/ · https://www.forbes.com/sites/chunkamui/2012/01/18/how-kodak-failed/

- Forked the whole editor instead of shipping a plugin. $1M→$500M ARR in ~24 months; $1B ARR Nov 2025, $2B Feb 2026 (company-reported — label as self-reported). Copilot 20M+ users per MSFT FY2025 10-K.
